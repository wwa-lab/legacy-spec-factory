## Relationship Candidates

**Relationship candidates** are potential foreign key patterns inferred from:
- DDS JFILE/JFLD (join logical file definitions)
- DB2 FOREIGN KEY constraints
- Program analysis showing lookups or cross-file reads
- SME notes on data relationships

**Status Legend**:
- `confirmed_from_code` - DDS JFLD or DB2 FOREIGN KEY/constraint explicitly
  supports the relationship
- `confirmed_by_sme` - SME confirms the relationship and evidence note is linked
- `strongly_inferred` - multiple evidence points support the candidate, but SME
  approval is still required before use as a fact
- `needs_sme_review` - plausible but unconfirmed
- `contradictory` - evidence disagrees and must be resolved before handoff

### Notes on Relationship Candidates

- **Naming conventions**: Some shops use naming patterns (e.g., CUSTNO in both CUSTOMER and ORDER files) to indicate relationships. These are candidates only; they must be confirmed against actual join logic in logical files, DB2 constraints, program CHAIN/SETLL operations, or SME approval.
- **Join logical files**: If an LF uses JFILE and JFLD to join two or more files, the JFLD declaration supports a `confirmed_from_code` relationship claim.
- **Program usage**: Program analyses may reveal repeated SETLL or CHAIN operations across files. These are `strongly_inferred` or `needs_sme_review` unless also confirmed by DDS or DB2 constraints.
- **Archival considerations**: Some relationships may become stale (e.g., historical order-to-customer joins after archival).
